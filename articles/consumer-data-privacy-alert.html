Consumer Data Privacy Alert: Protecting Personal Information

Category: Privacy · Updated recently

Many scams and account takeovers start with exposed personal data—old passwords, public profiles, data broker listings, or reused logins. Here’s a quick, practical checklist to reduce risk.

Quick privacy wins

  • Turn on 2-factor authentication (2FA) for email and banking
  • Use a password manager and unique passwords
  • Limit what you share publicly on social media
  • Review app permissions and remove what you don’t need

Reduce exposure

  • Opt out of data brokers when possible
  • Use email aliases for sign-ups
  • Disable “public profile” settings on accounts you don’t use
  • Set bank alerts for logins and transactions

Note: Informational only; not legal advice.
