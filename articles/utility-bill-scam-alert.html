Utility Bill Scam Alert: “Disconnect Notice” Payment Demands

Category: Scams · Updated recently

Utility scams often claim your power/water/gas will be shut off unless you pay immediately. Scammers use urgency, spoofed caller IDs, and unusual payment methods to pressure you.

Common warning signs

  • Threats of immediate shutoff “within 30 minutes”
  • Requests to pay with gift cards, crypto, or wire transfer
  • Refusal to let you call back on the official utility number
  • Spelling mistakes or strange links in emails/texts

What to do

  • Hang up and call your utility using the number on your bill or official website.
  • Do not pay via gift cards, crypto, or “cash apps” requested by the caller.
  • Check your online account directly for balance/alerts.
  • Report the attempt to your utility and your local consumer protection office.
