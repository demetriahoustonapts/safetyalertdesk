Social Media Scam Warning: Fake Accounts and Support Impersonators

Category: Scams · Updated recently

Scammers are impersonating friends, brands, and “support teams” on social platforms to steal accounts, money, or personal information. These scams often start with a believable message and a profile that looks real at first glance.

A common pattern is: a fake account messages you → you’re asked to “verify” something → you’re sent a link to a look-alike login page → your password or verification code gets captured → your account is taken over.

What’s happening

  • Impostor accounts copy real profiles and message followers.
  • Fake “support” claims your account is locked or violating rules.
  • Links go to look-alike sites that steal your password or codes.
  • Once taken over, your account is used to scam your friends.

Red flags / warning signs

  • Unexpected messages with urgency: “act now” / “final warning.”
  • Requests for your password, 2FA code, or recovery code.
  • Links that don’t match the platform’s official domain.
  • Payment requests to “restore” or “verify” your account.
  • Profiles with recent creation dates or strange follower patterns.

What to do right now

  • Do not click the link. Go directly to the platform app/website.
  • Change your password (use a unique, strong one).
  • Turn on 2FA and save backup codes somewhere secure.
  • Review “Logged in devices” and log out of unknown sessions.
  • Warn friends if your account posted odd messages.
Pro tip: Real platform support will not ask for your password or one-time codes by DM. If someone asks for codes, assume it’s a scam.
