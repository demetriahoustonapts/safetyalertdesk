Text Message Scam Alert: Fake Delivery Notices Are Increasing

What’s happening: Consumers across the U.S. are reporting an increase in fake text messages claiming to be from delivery companies. These messages often say a package is delayed or needs confirmation and include a link.

The messages are designed to look urgent and legitimate, frequently using familiar brand names or generic phrases like “delivery issue” or “address problem.”

Who is affected

Anyone who regularly shops online may receive these messages. Scammers do not need personal information to send them and often blast them out to thousands of phone numbers at once.

What the messages look like

  • Claims a package cannot be delivered
  • Includes a shortened or unfamiliar link
  • Creates urgency (“respond today”)
  • Requests confirmation or payment

What you should do

  • Do not click links in unexpected delivery texts
  • Check your order directly through the retailer’s official website
  • Delete the message immediately
  • Report the scam to your mobile carrier

Why this matters

Clicking scam links can lead to identity theft, unauthorized charges, or malware installation. Awareness is the best protection.

Reminder: Legitimate delivery companies do not ask for sensitive information via text.
